me2day
  • me2day is a micro-blog service (South Korea’s Twitter equivalent) where users can enter 150 letters to share information on their daily life with friends and families. The 150 letter ‘small talk’ breaks the barrier between the Internet and mobile environment and is helping me2day solidify a position as the next-generation network service leader. I mainly worked on me2day version 3.0 as a lead UX researcher who is responsible for interaction design (main duty), UI design (sub duty).

  • Why me2day 3.0
  • The increase of existing users' service loyalty was the essential goal of me2day ver 3.0. This utmost user loyalty was neccesary for a constant growth of service because the user satisfaction directly increases the number of potential users by means of inviting thier friends into the service. In 2011, the total me2day members exceeded 3 million, which was a high time to improve the service on a large scale so that me2day could be stated as a lead communication platform.
